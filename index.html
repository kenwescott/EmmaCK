My UI/UX journey...

During this semester, I'll be taking my first steps as a UI designer. I've enrolled in a course called SEG 3125- Analysis and Design of User Interfaces, and I'm excited (and a little nervous) to dive in. Just to set the scene: a User Interface (UI) is basically the point of interaction between a human and a digital product—like the buttons you click, the forms you fill out, or the layout of a website. It's what you see and touch when using software. Now, that's not to be confused with User Experience (UX), which is broader.UX is how the whole play makes you feel. To improve how users interact with software, designers use a blend of tools: colors, contrast, typography, spacing, icons, and more. It's all about making digital experiences not just usable, but pleasant and engaging too.
